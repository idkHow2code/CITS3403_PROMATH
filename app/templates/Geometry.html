What is π?

π (spelled as pi) is the circumference of any circle to the diameter of that circle. Regardless of the circle's size, this ratio will always equal pi.

To be exact

π = 3.1415926535

But this is too long to remember... Hence, we should always round it up to 3d.p

π ≈ 3.142

In summary, expect any shapes with a circle to have a π in it's formula

Angles

Now, we will learn about angles in a 2D shape.

In every shape, no matter how many sides or how complicated the shape is:

Total Angle inside a Shape is always

360o

The formula:

(n−2) × 180o

Every Triangle has 3 sides, n = 3
Total angle in a Triangle= (3-2) x 180o = 180o
Every Square has 4 sides, n = 4
Total angle in a Square= (4-2) x 180o = 360o
Every Pentagon has 5 sides, n = 5
Total angle in a Pentagon= (5-2) x 180o = 540o
Every Hexagon has 6 sides, n = 6
Total angle in a Hexagon= (6-2) x 180o = 720o

You may wonder, does the size of the shape affect the angles?

The answer is:

NO
